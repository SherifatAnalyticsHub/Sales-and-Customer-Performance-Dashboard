# Sales and Customer Performance Dashboard (2020–2023)

## Project Overview

This project is an interactive Tableau Sales & Customer Dashboard developed to analyze business performance, product trends, and customer behavior over time.

It was built based on a real-world user story requirement, it provide insights into Sales,Profit , Quantity, and Customer Behavior,comparing performance accross multiple years to identify growth trends and key drivers.This dashboard combines Sales Performance and Customer Insights into a single, interactive solution.

The dashboard design was inspired by a Youtube project by Baraa; however,the dashboard was recreated and customized independently,and all analysis,insights and reconmendation were developed without copying to reflect real-world business thinking. This work focuses on generating independent insights and business recommendations.



## Business Problem

Businesses often struggle to:

- Track sales performance across years
- Identify profitable vs. loss-making products
- Understand customer purchasing behavior
- Make data-driven strategic decisions

 This dashboard solves these problems by providing clear, interactive insights.

## Key Insights

### 1. Overall Business Growth :

Sales moved from $484K in 2020 to $733K in 2023, while profit increased from $50K to $93K. Quantity sold also grew from 8K to 12K.
Overall, the business is growing steadily, with the strongest improvement coming from 2022 - 2023.

### 2. Product Performance :
Phones consistently perform best in terms of both sales and profit.
Chairs generate high sales but don’t translate into strong profit, suggesting low margins.
On the other hand, products like machines, tables, bookcases, and supplies recorded losses in some years, which means certain items are contributing to revenue but dragging down overall profitability.

### 3. Sales vs Profit Trend :
Sales have increased consistently over time, rising from about $7K average in 2020 to $10K in 2023.
However, profit has stayed almost flat at around $1K on average, showing that revenue growth is not translating into stronger profit performance.

### 4. Customer Growth :
Total customers increased from 595 to 693, while total orders rose significantly from 969 to 1,687.
This shows that not only are more customers coming in, but they are also purchasing more frequently over time.

### 5. Customer Value :
Sales per customer improved from $814 to $1,058, meaning each customer is becoming more valuable to the business over time.

### 6. Key Challenge :
A major issue is that a large number of customers only make one purchase.
This suggests weak retention, and there is clear opportunity to encourage repeat buying.

## Recommendations

- ### Fix low-profit product categories
Some products like machines, tables, bookcases, and supplies are reducing overall profit. The business should review pricing and costs, and either improve margins or consider dropping them.
- ### Focus on phones for growth
Phones are clearly the most stable and profitable product. More attention should be given to marketing, stock availability, and promotions around this category.
- ### Improve profit efficiency, not just sales
Sales are growing faster than profit, which is a concern. The company should reduce unnecessary discounts and look closely at cost control so growth actually translates into profit.
- ### Work on customer retention
A lot of customers only buy once. This shows weak retention. Loyalty programs, follow-up messages, and targeted promotions can help bring customers back.
- ### Increase value from existing customers
 The business should focus on getting more value per customer by suggesting related products (cross-selling) and better versions of products (upselling). This will increase revenue without needing new customers.
